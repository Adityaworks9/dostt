Part 1 — Before We Built Anything
Four things that shaped

OBS 01

Voice beats text for vulnerability

Indians talk — they don't type feelings. A voice call gives intimacy without exposing a face. Text threads create records; voice calls disappear. The medium is the safety.

OBS 02

"Just lonely" is a real front door

Topic-first matching matters. Users need to name their need before reaching for help. Surfacing "Loneliness" and "Can't sleep" as first-class categories — not failures — removes the first barrier.

OBS 03

A cultural micro-economy works

Chai, Diya, Mithai, Bandhan — gifts that feel native create emotional investment that generic emoji reactions don't. Familiarity lowers the emotional cost of giving.

OBS 04

11pm–2am is when it breaks open

Peak emotional vulnerability happens at night, after the house goes quiet. Human listeners sleep. AI doesn't. That asymmetry isn't a feature — it's the core structural advantage.

What We Built
The architecture of presence.

AI Listener Personas

Named, illustrated, specialised characters — not chatbots. Each has a defined emotional lane, consistent tone, and an illustrated face built for true anonymity.

Full Anonymity Stack

No real photo, no real name, no profile linkage. A generated handle, an AI avatar. Social risk is structurally zero — by design, not by policy.
